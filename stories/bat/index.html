Thunk.

I flinch as drops of warm blood splatter on my face.

Thunk.

…

Thunk.

It stops moving.


“Wow,” I stammer after a moment, wiping my face. “That was… something.”

The person standing opposite of me nods, shrugs. He’s slightly taller, slightly older, but not by much. Kind face, hazel eyes, messy black hair, dark blue sweater — not the type of guy you expect to beat something to death with a baseball bat.

We stand in a white plane of nothingness, a bloody corpse apart; a red spatter on an endless blank canvas. The creature was a repulsive one, even before getting its head smashed in — wolf-like, with a convulsed spine and a pathetic excuse of a skin stretched over its lumpy flesh. It had been surprisingly fast too; I had been [second instance of ‘had been’ in sentence — awkward?] on the run for days now.

“Here, take this,” he says, handing me the bloodied bat. “I think you may need it sometime again. The demons follow everyone.”

I take the bat awkwardly, almost dropping it as its entire weight is passed into my hand. “Thanks,” I reply. It wasn’t a lie; I really might be needing it again. This wasn’t the first monster who had followed me before, and it surely wasn’t going to be the last. I had outrun all of them so far, but having a defence was comforting.

“Won’t you need it though?” I ask.

He gives me a small smile before shaking his head. “Ah, I appreciate the sentiment, but don’t worry about me; I am fully capable of handling myself.”


We stand in silence for a few more moments.


“Thanks again for, you know,” I say, gesturing to the corpse.

“Any time, friend,” he replies, turning away.

I hesitate, then call out. “Wait! What’s your name?”
