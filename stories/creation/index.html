Creation

A Creator lumbers through the forest, eyes blank, chest heavy, arms crimson. The fog parts and the air buzzes as he passes through, leaving an unsettling stillness in his wake. Old leaves crunch beneath his feet, sending jarring echoes through the surrounding woods.

Dark, casual clothes hang on his slender figure, mirroring his face — dull and empty. Cold tears stream from his eyes, pooling on his chin. He doesn’t bother to wipe them away.


The Creator stops and takes an absent look around. Creations frolic about in the distance, dancing, running, flying, laughing, adding vibrant splashes of colour to the otherwise monotone surroundings.

A spark flashes across his eye, and he almost smiles. It only lasts for a split second, though, and then he’s back to his usual self as if it never happened.


He remembers what he is here to do, and sorrow stabs him through the heart. He jolts, shudders and collapses to his knees, clutching at his chest. More tears pour down his face, but they too are ignored.

He has a job to do, however. Gritting his teeth, he stands back up. With a sharp inhale through the nose, he grasps the hilt of sorrow’s knife and yanks it out of his chest. His torso begins to run warm and sticky, but it doesn’t hurt now.


A Creation swoops past. It is fast, just like he designed it, but the Creator is faster still. Cold fingers close on its leg, and it gives a small yip as it’s yanked out of the air.

He holds it up in front of him, examining it, remembering. He remembers making it, the joy it gave him when it first opened its eyes. He remembers fixing it when it came running back hurt, bawling. He remembers comforting it when it was too shy to play with the others.


All his Creations were like that. But no longer. Now, they are just bitter reminders of a time passed.

He readjusts his grip on the Creation, and it does not resist. It only stares innocently, patiently. Its gaze does not falter even when the blade is raised to its neck.

Can it feel it? Can it feel the death by the edge?


The Creator pauses, as he always does.


Why is he doing this? Why throw it all away? Isn’t there a better way?


Can he not change anymore?


He shakes his head in a futile attempt to clear it. He doesn’t want to answer those questions.


Push in, tug firmly to the side. The movement is fluid, familiar. He releases the body, blood trickling down his forearms, adding yet another layer to the dried coat already there.
